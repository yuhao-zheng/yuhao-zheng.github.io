Because of the complex ownership of the land of urban villages, it is hard to renew urban village as a whole. In order to guarantee the beauty of the city, developers using the strategy of “surround” and “cover”, hiding the urban village behind the high-rise with the commercial podium and residential tower. This kind of city renewal pattern ignored the need for people live in urban village and make their bad living condition even worse. It also ruined the original transportation and commercial system of the village.

Based on Jane Jacobs’s theory, we separated the building into three parts, SOHO and commercial zone, park, and residential zone. SOHO zone offer nature surveillance and commercial activities to the village society, high-line Park provide a buffer zone between two zones and green space for them to take a rest. The residential zone provides the apartments to support city development. Buyers can plug in the apartment they choose into the building.

Buyer can design their own houses by choosing different boxes as they like. Besides, the concept of half-box encouraged the users to maintain the courtyard system of the building. Buyers can buy half box of the house and they will be able to have a half house-half court-yard. Here are some examples of it.
